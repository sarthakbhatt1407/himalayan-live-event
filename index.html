Overview Redefining Indian Healthcare Ecosystem

The recently-held second Global Investors Summit in Dehradun, Uttarakhand, witnessed investment proposals worth more than Rs 3.5 lakh being received by the hill state. Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) to the tune of Rs 44,0000 crore have already got inked.

With healthcare and pharma emerging as one of the focus sectors, the vertical is clearly at an inflection point. However, before the sector moves to the next level of growth, it has to overcome several existing bottlenecks endemic to the state. There is an acute shortage of doctors and health personnel, especially in the hilly districts. Their retention is an even bigger challenge. The high maternal mortality and child mortality rates have to be reduced. The soaring incidence of noncommunicable and communicable diseases has to be brought under control and above all, ensure universal health coverage.
